## 1. Introduction
Networks are classified primarily based on the **geographical area** they cover.

## 2. Network Types

### A. PAN (Personal Area Network)
* **Range:** Smallest (10–100 meters).
* **Usage:** Personal workspace (connecting phone to laptop, smartwatch).
* **Tech:** Bluetooth, Zigbee, USB.
* **Status:** Private, High Speed, Lowest Cost.

### B. LAN (Local Area Network)
* **Range:** Small (Up to 2 km).
* **Usage:** Single building, office, school lab, or home.
* **Tech:** Ethernet (Cables), Wi-Fi.
* **Status:** Private, High Speed, Low Error Rate.

### C. CAN (Campus Area Network)
* **Range:** Moderate (1–5 km).
* **Usage:** Interconnecting multiple LANs within a specific boundary like a **University** or **Corporate Campus**.
* **Status:** Private, Moderate Cost & Maintenance.

### D. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)
* **Range:** City-wide (5–50 km).
* **Usage:** Cable TV networks, City-wide broadband.
* **Tech:** Fiber Optics (FDDI), ATM.
* **Status:** Private or Public, Moderate Speed, Difficult Maintenance.

### E. WAN (Wide Area Network)
* **Range:** Largest (Countries/Continents).
* **Usage:** The **Internet**, connecting offices in different countries.
* **Tech:** Leased Lines, Satellites, 4G/5G.
* **Status:** Public, Lowest relative speed, Highest Error Rate & Cost.
